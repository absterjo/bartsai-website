About Barts.AI

Barts.AI is the artificial intelligence and data science team embedded within the Barts Life Sciences group at Barts Health NHS Trust. We work across specialties with clinicians, operational leaders and researchers to develop and evaluate AI tools that address real problems in patient care and hospital workflows.

Our mission is to move from experimental algorithms to safe, robust AI-enabled services. We place patient benefit, clinical safety, transparency and fairness at the centre of everything we do, and we build in close partnership with the Trust, Barts Life Sciences, academic collaborators and patients.

Our focus areas

  • Clinical decision support, including risk prediction and triage tools
  • Imaging and signal analysis (for example radiology and pathology)
  • Operational analytics, such as patient flow and theatre utilisation
  • Research collaborations with universities and industry partners

Team & collaboration

The Barts.AI team brings together clinicians, data scientists, analysts and engineers who understand both the clinical context and the technical detail. We work closely with digital, information governance, research and quality improvement teams across the Trust.

We collaborate with clinical teams who have ideas for using AI in their service, as well as academic and industry partners working on methods that could benefit our patients. We are especially interested in projects with clear pathways to safe deployment and measurable improvement in outcomes or experience.

Who we work with

  • Clinical teams seeking to evaluate AI tools in practice
  • Researchers looking for real-world data and clinical input
  • Industry partners developing AI that needs robust clinical evaluation

Governance & ethics

All Barts.AI projects follow Barts Health NHS Trust processes for information governance, research and innovation. We work with Trust governance teams to ensure appropriate approvals, data protection and oversight are in place before data are accessed or tools are tested in practice.

  • Compliance with NHS and Trust policies on data protection and information security
  • Appropriate ethical or service evaluation approvals for research and pilot work
  • Engagement with clinicians, and where appropriate patients and the public, when designing and evaluating AI tools
